Vector Embeddings in Natural Language Processing

Vector embeddings are dense, continuous numerical representations of discrete objects such as words, sentences, or documents. In the context of natural language processing, embeddings map linguistic units into a high-dimensional vector space where geometric relationships between vectors capture semantic and syntactic similarities between the corresponding linguistic entities. This representation is fundamental to nearly all modern NLP systems, enabling models to reason about language in a mathematically structured way.

The concept of word embeddings gained widespread attention with the introduction of Word2Vec by Mikolov et al. in 2013, which demonstrated that words appearing in similar contexts tend to have similar vector representations. The famous example that "king minus man plus woman approximately equals queen" illustrated how embeddings capture meaningful relationships. GloVe, developed at Stanford, extended this idea by incorporating global word co-occurrence statistics, while FastText from Facebook improved upon these methods by representing words as compositions of character n-grams, enabling better handling of rare and out-of-vocabulary words.

With the rise of Transformer-based models, the field moved from static embeddings, where each word has a single fixed vector regardless of context, to contextual embeddings. Models like BERT, GPT, and their successors produce different vector representations for the same word depending on its surrounding context. For example, the word "bank" would receive different embeddings in the sentences "river bank" and "savings bank." This contextual sensitivity dramatically improved performance across a range of NLP tasks.

Sentence and document-level embeddings have also become increasingly important, particularly in applications like semantic search, clustering, and retrieval-augmented generation. Models such as Sentence-BERT and Instructor produce fixed-length vectors for entire sentences or passages, enabling efficient similarity comparisons. These embeddings are typically stored in vector databases like FAISS, Pinecone, or Weaviate, which support fast approximate nearest neighbor search. The quality of vector embeddings directly impacts downstream task performance, making embedding model selection and fine-tuning critical steps in any NLP pipeline.
